Frutales-specific verbs:
- Fertilizá trimestral
- Podá formativa (formative pruning, first 2 years)
- Cosechá maduro (harvest at full ripeness)
- Injertá / Injerto (graft — a referenced concept, not necessarily a user action)
- Trasplantá con cuidado (transplant carefully — citrus dislike root disturbance)

Frutales-specific climate cues:
- "subtropical / templado-cálido" (citrus comfort zone)
- "helada fuerte" (citrus-killing frost; <-3°C threshold)
- "primera cosecha en 2-4 años" (patience)
- "sin embolar" (without bagging — for higuera against birds)

Suculentas-specific lexicon:
- "metabolismo CAM" (Crassulacean Acid Metabolism — THE keyword)
- "tejido suculento / hojas suculentas / tallos suculentos"
- "drenaje crítico" / "tierra arenosa" / "perlita / piedra pómez"
- "estiolación" (leggy stretching from low light) — when "se estira buscando luz"
- "época seca de invierno" (winter drought rest)

Specific frutales whyRationale anchors:
- aguacate: Lauraceae family, Mexico origin, persistent (no monthly cycle), needs deep soil + good drainage; flowers are cross-pollinator (A type vs B type)
- higuera: Moraceae, Mediterranean, deciduous in winter, fruits twice (breba spring + main summer), tolerates poor soil
- limonero: Rutaceae, perpetual flowering/fruiting (less seasonal than naranjo); cold-sensitive
- mandarino + naranjo: Rutaceae, seasonal heavy fruiting; same care as limonero but earlier harvest window for mandarino

Specific suculentas whyRationale anchors:
- cactus (Cactaceae): family-wide CAM metabolism, areolas (where spines grow), waxy cuticle, native arid Americas
- echeveria (Crassulaceae): rosette form, Mexican origin, CAM, pulvinate leaves
- haworthia (Asphodelaceae): South African, tolerates low-medium light better than echeveria/cactus, translucent leaf "windows" allow sub-photosynthesis
- sedum (Crassulaceae): cold-hardier than echeveria, ground-cover varieties for outdoor
- suculenta-generica: catch-all; whyRationale should be FAMILY-LEVEL CAM mechanism without committing to species specifics
